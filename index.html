Glucose Stabilizing Herbal Capsules For Diabetics

Diabetes mellitus, commonly known as diabetes, is a lifelong condition that affects many people and can cause stress. This study aimed to develop a herbal medicine that could lower blood sugar levels in people with diabetes. The study employed the R&D (research and development) method using the ADDIE model, specifically the analyse, design and develop stages, based on qualitative data from development outcomes and literature reviews.

The development process began with an analysis of the ingredients used: butterfly pea flowers, rose flowers and spirulina, which were encapsulated in seaweed capsules. The capsule filling was then formulated with a ratio of rose flowers to hibiscus flowers to spirulina of 1:2:3. Corn starch was added as an excipient at 10% of the total mixed ingredients. The development process began with the extraction and mixing of the ingredients and the addition of the corn starch as an excipient. This was then encapsulated into size 0 capsules using a capsule filling machine.

This study concludes that GLUCOCARE is a herbal diabetes medication in capsule form, made from rose and hibiscus flowers and spirulina, with capsule shells derived from seaweed. It can be used to control blood sugar levels in patients with diabetes.

Extraction Process

Ingredients are ground into powder and extracted with ethanol (1:5) for 2 days, then filtered.

Evaporation Process

The extract is evaporated for 1 hour at 60 °C, rested, mixed with corn starch, and oven-dried for 48 hours.

Mixing

Rose, butterfly pea, spirulina, and corn starch powders are blended according to the formulation.

Capsule Filling

The mixture is filled into 500 mg capsules (1:2:3 ratio) with 10% corn starch.
